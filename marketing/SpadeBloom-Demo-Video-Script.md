# SpadeBloom — 90-Second Demo Video Script

**Goal:** Show, in 90 seconds, that SpadeBloom makes the floor visible in real time, captures labor automatically, and — the hero moment — lets operators inspect assemblies in 3D/AR. End on a clear demo CTA.

**Audience:** owners, plant/ops managers and QA leads at metal-fabrication shops.
**Tone:** confident, plain-spoken, "made by people who've stood on a floor." Not salesy.
**Format:** screen-capture of the product + short live-action b-roll of a real shop + operator phone/tablet. Mostly product.
**Music:** clean, driving but understated; lifts at the 3D/AR moment, settles for the CTA.
**Voice:** one calm narrator (VO). On-screen text reinforces each beat.
**Runtime:** ~90s. The 3D/AR QA beat is the centerpiece (~28s of the 90).

> **Honesty guardrails for the edit:** label the metric "Labor efficiency," never "OEE." Don't show or imply materials/scheduling/costing. Show 3D/AR QA exactly as it works (view assembly vs. model, pin defects, AR at the station, sign-off). Seed the demo with a realistic, populated floor — never all-zero dashboards.

## Production notes

- **Total VO word count:** ~150 words → comfortable at a calm 90s pace. Trim the 0:00 line first if you run long.
- **The 3D/AR beat must breathe.** Give the model rotate and the AR overlay real screen time — it's the moment people remember. Don't rush the pin-drop.
- **Capture at 1080p/60fps** for smooth UI motion; record the phone separately and composite.
- **Captions/subtitles:** burn in the on-screen text lines above; ~85% of social video is watched muted, so the story must read without sound.
- **Cutdowns to also export:**
  - **15s teaser** (for ads / LinkedIn): 0:36–1:04 (the 3D/AR moment) + the CTA card.
  - **6s bumper:** the AR overlay + pin-drop, end card.
  - **Square (1:1) and vertical (9:16)** crops for social; keep the dashboard and model centered.
- **End card holds 3s** so viewers can read the URL.
- **Alt CTA card** (if used for outbound email): "Reply for a 20-minute walkthrough."
