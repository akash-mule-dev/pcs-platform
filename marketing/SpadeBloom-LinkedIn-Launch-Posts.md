## Post 2 — The 3D/AR QA differentiator (Day 3–4)
*Format: short clip of the AR inspection (the 15s cutdown) or the 3D/AR slide.*

Most shops still check a welded assembly against a 2D drawing on a clipboard.

A flat drawing can't really tell you if the built part matches intent. So defects get caught late, recorded inconsistently, and the same mistake quietly repeats.

Here's the part of SpadeBloom I'm most proud of:

The operator opens the actual 3D model — on a tablet, or in AR right at the station — and inspects the real assembly against it. They pin each defect to the exact spot on the geometry: type, severity, measurement, tolerance. It's signed off, and recurring issues show up as a pattern instead of disappearing into a binder.

Fewer escapes. Less rework. A quality record tied to the real part.

(Deeper scan-to-model comparison is what we're building next.)

## Post 3 — Problem / insight, thought-leadership (Day 6–7)
*Format: text-only, or one simple graphic with the "12 hours" stat.*

A number that should bother every shop owner:

The data most planners use to make decisions at 9am is, at best, 12 hours old.

It's written on yesterday's travelers and end-of-shift spreadsheets. Paper works great when you've got six jobs open. At sixty, it becomes a liability — and you don't notice until a late delivery makes it obvious.

The fix usually pitched is a full ERP. But ERPs take quarters to roll out, cost a fortune, and — the part nobody admits — the floor often still doesn't use them.

What a floor actually needs first is simpler: a live answer to "who's on what, against target, right now," and labor that gets captured without anyone filling out a form.

Get that, and costing, quoting and scheduling all get better — because they're finally built on real numbers.

That's the gap we built SpadeBloom for. Happy to compare notes if you're wrestling with this.

## Posting tips
- **Cadence:** ~3–4 days apart so they don't cannibalize each other; Tue–Thu mornings tend to perform best for B2B.
- **First two lines are everything** — that's all that shows before "…see more." Keep the hook tight.
- **Native video/images only.** LinkedIn suppresses posts that link out; put the URL in the post text and/or first comment, not as a link preview.
- **Reply to every comment** in the first 2 hours — it drives reach more than anything else.
- **Tag** a couple of relevant people/partners where it's genuine, not spammy.
- **Hashtags:** 3–5, mixing broad (#manufacturing) and niche (#metalfabrication, #welding).
- **Repurpose:** Post 3 (the insight) also works as a short article; Posts 2 and 5 work as X/Twitter and as cold-email openers.
